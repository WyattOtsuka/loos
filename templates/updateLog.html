2020 Ride Superpig

Size: 154cm

Rider Weight: 160 lbs

Rider Height: 6'4"

Preffered Style: Freeride

The 2020 Ride Superpig is one of my all time favorite boards. Given my height, and my shoe size, it made a lot of sense for me to get a volume shifted board. While my weight would put me on a 150 cm, I went with the 154, simply because a 150 is really short for my height, and having that extra tail is really useful for me.

What makes the Superpig so great is how great of an all around daily driver this is. It does absolutely fantastic carving with the stiff an snappy tail, and can be put on a super agressive edge with how wide the board is. The camber gives this board very locked in feel, and the progressive sidecut allows for a very easy entry into the turn, and a quick and snappy exit. For powder, this board is a super fun ride. The volume shifted nature of the board really helps it float, although in the wet and heavy powder we get in the PNW, it does leave a bit to be desired. When it floats, it floats great, but when it doesn't, it'll take you down with it. You need to be a bit more mindful about powder riding, but it's a lot of fun with the quick and snappy profile of the board. The K2 Excavator or Ride Psychocandy would be slightly better option if you find yourself riding powder more often than not. The Superpig also matches my apptitude for freestyle riding, which is to say, lacking. If you want a board that would do better on rails, then there's always the Warpig. Howerver, if you're looking to jump, the carbon in this board is INSANE for providing pop and power. While the tail may be short, it is extremely lively and ready to send you to the moon. The nose is a lot of fun too, as its far more flexy, and allows for some decent nose presses for how aggressive the board really is.

All in all, the Superpig is a fantastic board, and a great choice for bigger guys who want a shorter board, and are looking for some hard riding out of the park. A very similar board would be the Gnu Gremlin. For more powder oriented riding, the Ride Psychocandy and K2 Excavator are great alternatives, and the Ride Warpig and Twinpig for a more park focused rider.
